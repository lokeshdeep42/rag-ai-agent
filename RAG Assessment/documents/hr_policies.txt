1. LEAVE POLICIES
=================

1.1 Annual Leave
----------------
All full-time employees are entitled to annual paid leave as follows:

- Employees with 0-2 years of service: 15 days per year
- Employees with 2-5 years of service: 20 days per year
- Employees with 5+ years of service: 25 days per year

Annual leave must be requested at least 2 weeks in advance through the HR portal. Leave requests are subject to manager approval based on business needs.

Unused leave can be carried forward to the next year, up to a maximum of 10 days. Any leave beyond this limit will be forfeited.

1.2 Sick Leave
--------------
Employees are entitled to 12 days of paid sick leave per year. Sick leave does not accumulate and cannot be carried forward.

For sick leave exceeding 3 consecutive days, a medical certificate must be submitted to HR within 2 working days of return.

1.3 Parental Leave
------------------
- Maternity Leave: 16 weeks of paid leave
- Paternity Leave: 2 weeks of paid leave
- Adoption Leave: 12 weeks of paid leave

Parental leave must be notified to HR at least 4 weeks in advance, except in emergency situations.

1.4 Bereavement Leave
---------------------
Employees are entitled to 5 days of paid bereavement leave in the event of death of an immediate family member (spouse, parent, child, sibling).

1.5 Public Holidays
-------------------
The company observes all national public holidays. A list of public holidays is published at the beginning of each year.

2. BENEFITS AND COMPENSATION
=============================

2.1 Health Insurance
--------------------
The company provides comprehensive health insurance coverage for all full-time employees and their immediate family members (spouse and children).

Coverage includes:
- Hospitalization and surgery
- Outpatient consultations
- Dental care (up to $2,000 per year)
- Vision care (up to $500 per year)
- Mental health services

Employees can enroll in the health insurance plan during onboarding or during the annual open enrollment period.

2.2 Retirement Benefits
-----------------------
The company contributes 10% of base salary to the employee's retirement fund. Employees can make additional voluntary contributions up to 15% of their salary.

Retirement benefits vest after 2 years of continuous employment.

2.3 Performance Bonus
---------------------
Eligible employees may receive an annual performance bonus based on individual and company performance. The bonus is typically paid in Q1 of the following year.

Bonus amounts range from 0% to 20% of annual base salary, depending on performance ratings.

2.4 Professional Development
----------------------------
The company provides an annual professional development budget of $2,000 per employee for:
- Training courses and certifications
- Conference attendance
- Professional memberships
- Educational materials

Employees must obtain manager approval before incurring any professional development expenses.

2.5 Remote Work Allowance
-------------------------
Employees working remotely are entitled to a monthly allowance of $100 to cover home office expenses such as internet, electricity, and office supplies.

3. CODE OF CONDUCT
==================

3.1 Professional Behavior
-------------------------
All employees are expected to maintain professional behavior at all times. This includes:

- Treating colleagues, clients, and partners with respect
- Maintaining confidentiality of sensitive information
- Avoiding conflicts of interest
- Complying with all company policies and procedures

3.2 Anti-Discrimination and Harassment
--------------------------------------
The company has zero tolerance for discrimination or harassment of any kind. All employees have the right to work in an environment free from discrimination based on race, gender, age, religion, disability, sexual orientation, or any other protected characteristic.

Any incidents of discrimination or harassment should be reported immediately to HR or through the anonymous ethics hotline.

3.3 Confidentiality
-------------------
Employees must not disclose confidential company information to unauthorized parties. This includes:
- Customer data
- Financial information
- Product roadmaps
- Strategic plans
- Proprietary technology

Confidentiality obligations continue even after employment ends.

3.4 Social Media Policy
-----------------------
Employees are free to use social media but must not:
- Share confidential company information
- Make disparaging comments about the company, colleagues, or clients
- Represent personal opinions as company positions

When in doubt, consult with HR before posting company-related content.

4. WORK HOURS AND ATTENDANCE
=============================

4.1 Standard Work Hours
-----------------------
Standard work hours are 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday through Friday, with a one-hour lunch break. This represents a 40-hour work week.

4.2 Flexible Work Arrangements
------------------------------
The company supports flexible work arrangements, including:
- Flexible start/end times (core hours: 10:00 AM - 4:00 PM)
- Remote work (up to 3 days per week, subject to manager approval)
- Compressed work weeks (subject to business needs)

4.3 Overtime
------------
Non-exempt employees are eligible for overtime pay at 1.5x their regular hourly rate for hours worked beyond 40 hours per week.

Overtime must be pre-approved by the employee's manager.

4.4 Attendance and Punctuality
------------------------------
Employees are expected to maintain good attendance and punctuality. Excessive absences or tardiness may result in disciplinary action.

If unable to attend work, employees must notify their manager as soon as possible, preferably before the start of the workday.

5. PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT
==========================

5.1 Performance Reviews
-----------------------
All employees undergo formal performance reviews twice per year:
- Mid-year review (June/July)
- Annual review (December/January)

Performance is evaluated based on:
- Achievement of goals and objectives
- Demonstration of company values
- Technical skills and competencies
- Collaboration and teamwork

5.2 Performance Improvement Plans
---------------------------------
Employees who do not meet performance expectations may be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP). The PIP outlines specific areas for improvement and provides a timeframe (typically 60-90 days) for the employee to demonstrate improvement.

5.3 Promotions and Career Development
-------------------------------------
The company is committed to promoting from within whenever possible. Promotion decisions are based on:
- Performance history
- Demonstrated leadership potential
- Skills and competencies required for the new role
- Business needs

Employees are encouraged to discuss career development goals with their managers during performance reviews.
